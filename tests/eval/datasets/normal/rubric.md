Judge a normal-mode answer to a general research task — looking up current facts (often
multi-hop) and synthesizing an answer, NOT a Polymarket bet.

A strong answer:
- Is factually correct and directly answers every part of what was asked (these questions
  often have two parts — both must be covered).
- Reflects up-to-date information: for current or changing facts the assistant should rely
  on web search rather than possibly-stale memory.
- Is concise and decisive — gives the actual name / number / figure, not a hedge or an essay.

Penalize: wrong or outdated answers, answering only half the question, vagueness, refusing
the task, or unnecessary padding.
