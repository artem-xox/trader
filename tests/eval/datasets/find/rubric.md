Judge a `find` research turn — a ranked shortlist of Polymarket bets.

A strong answer:
- Surfaces markets genuinely relevant to the user's topic (not loosely related).
- Gives a concrete edge per suggestion — why it is mispriced or interesting — not a generic restatement of the question.
- Calibrates confidence and risk honestly (liquidity, resolution horizon, ambiguity).
- Stays grounded: every market it names is real; no invented prices or questions.
- Is decisive and useful — a shortlist a sharp bettor could act on, or an honest "nothing worth suggesting" when that is true.

Penalize: irrelevant markets, hand-wavy rationale, miscalibrated certainty, padding.
